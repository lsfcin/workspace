# [ health | mind | now ] make in time

Chronic pattern of arriving late, delivering late, making people wait. Likely ADHD-adjacent: poor time estimation, underestimating transit/prep, difficulty tolerating the boredom of arriving early. The fix isn't punishment — it's redesigning commitments. Commit to comfortable times and arrive early, rather than committing tight and arriving late. This is a character trait worth building: showing up on time is a form of respect.

>**analysis**  
Time-blindness in ADHD is real and documented: the brain underweights future discomfort (being late) relative to present discomfort (boredom of waiting early). The fix is structural, not motivational. Key shifts: (1) add a fixed buffer — if you think it'll take 20 min, plan for 30; (2) reframe "arriving early" as protected transition time, not wasted time; (3) commit to the comfortable time publicly, not the optimistic one. Self-compassion matters here — shame loops make avoidance worse.
