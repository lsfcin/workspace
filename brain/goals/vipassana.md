# [ spiritual | mind | year ] vipassana

Not as self-care — as a natural part of routine. The motivation is clear: want the magic of the world, and perceiving it requires emotional openness, and emotional openness requires a less distracted mind. The mind is currently distracted. Meditation as training for perception, not productivity.

>**signals**  
transformative · optional · motivated

>**fears**  
*what · sitting in silence and finding only noise  
when · when the mind is very activated, or practice feels performative  
why · meditation's value is invisible at first; it's hard to trust before feeling it  
how · deferring practice until conditions feel "right" — which they never do*

>**analysis**  
Vipassana literally means "to see clearly." Its documented effect on attention and emotional regulation requires consistent, low-effort practice before big-effort practice. "Observar minhas tristezas com paciência" — this is already the practice described. The resistance usually isn't to meditation itself but to what gets revealed when the noise settles. Starting with 5 minutes of doing nothing (not guided, not structured) removes the identity overhead of "meditator" and lets the practice begin as simple stillness.

## selected next achievement
    [sit-5] sit in silence for 5 minutes — no guidance, no goal, just exist

**ease-start**  
Find a chair or cushion. Set a timer for 5 minutes. Close your eyes. Do nothing. Not "clear your mind" — just sit with whatever is there. Notice without fixing.

>**timing**  
*target · 10-minute daily practice within 3 months  
anchor · none external  
closure · meditation happens before checking the phone — it becomes a default  
tolerance · skipped days don't break the practice; the practice survives interruption  
fallback · even 2 minutes of intentional stillness counts*
