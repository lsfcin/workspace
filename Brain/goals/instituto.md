>**analysis**  
The fear is rational — it's the finishing problem applied to its highest stakes form. But the mind is using real self-knowledge as a veto on action, which is a different thing than prudence. "Tirar o peso da armadura do cavaleiro da honra" — the armor of obligation to never disappoint is heavier than the work itself. "Aceitar as rejeições com leveza e seguir" — this applies here too: even if something starts and doesn't finish as imagined, that's not betrayal. The intervention is scale-down commitment: don't start the institute — start a coffee conversation with one person on that list. The discipline question gets tested at micro scale first, not macro scale first. Fear of leadership correlates with fear of relational disappointment — not failure per se, but letting specific people down. Named board members make this concrete in a way that activates the fear strongly. First move: don't announce the board — just think together with one trusted person about what this could be.
